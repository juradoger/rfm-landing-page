Why Your Support Matters

The Red-fronted Macaw is a Critically Endangered parrot that only occurs in the Andean Dry Valleys of Bolivia, where the Red-fronted Macaw Community Reserve protects the world's most important breeding cliff.

This sanctuary holds over 25 breeding pairs who nest each year in rock cavities in sandstone cliffs, and harboring nearly 100 Red-fronted Macaws outside the breeding season.

Since 2006, this reserve has been protected in collaboration with the Indigenous Quechua communities of San Carlos, Perereta, and Amaya, forming the only community-led, privately managed sanctuary dedicated to this unique macaw.

By supporting this Reserve, you help protect a vast dry forest ecosystem that shelters more than 200 bird species — including the endemic Red-fronted Macaw, Bolivian Blackbird and Cliff Parakeet as well as the globally threatened, Andean Condor. Other emblematic wildlife such as the Puma, Culpeo Fox, Molina's Hog-nosed Skunk, and Geoffroy's Cat also find refuge here.

Observation Hides built to honor the Red-fronted Macaw

Another one of our achievements: launching and establishing the Observation Hide in the Red-fronted Macaw Reserve.

It all began with a simple yet powerful vision

To create a space where people can observe the Red-fronted Macaw without disturbing its natural world. A quiet hide that blends into the landscape, letting visitors appreciate these birds just as they are.

Crafted for immersive viewing

Rustic style, approx. 4 × 3 meters and 2.20 meters high, with six small viewing windows aligned to favorite perching spots so every guest can capture a memorable photograph.

Built by skilled hands, aimed at lasting impact

A team of naturalistic sculptors brought it to life—positioning the Reserve as a high-quality birdwatching destination that supports and benefits local communities.

Why Your Donation Makes a Real Impact

A monthly gift of $25, or even more, turns you into a direct conservation partner, creating lasting change in one of the most threatened landscapes on Earth. Your support can help us continue with:

Habitat Protection

Protection of nesting cliffs and habitats through direct collaboration with Quechua communities who steward the land and make key conservation decisions.

Forest Restoration

The establishment and maintenance of a 540,000 native tree nursery, restoring essential fruiting forests the macaws depend on.

Community Employment

Dignified local employment in ecotourism, honey production, and nursery work—offering sustainable alternatives to harmful land use and the illegal wildlife trade.
