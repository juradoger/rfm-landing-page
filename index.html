Why Your Support Matters

The Red-fronted Macaw (RFM) is a Critically Endangered parrot that only occurs in the Andean Dry Valleys of Bolivia, where the Red-fronted Macaw Community Reserve protects the world's most important breeding cliff.

This sanctuary holds over 25 breeding pairs who nest each year in rock cavities in sandstone cliffs, and harboring nearly 100 Red-fronted Macaws outside the breeding season. Since 2006, this reserve has been protected in collaboration with the Indigenous Quechua communities of San Carlos, Perereta, and Amaya, forming the only community-led, privately managed sanctuary dedicated to this unique macaw.

By supporting this Reserve, you help protect a vast dry forest ecosystem that shelters more than 200 bird species — including the endemic Red-fronted Macaw, Bolivian Blackbird and Cliff Parakeet as well as the globally threatened, Andean Condor. Other emblematic wildlife such as the Puma, Culpeo Fox, Molina's Hog-nosed Skunk, and Geoffroy's Cat also find refuge here.

Why Your Donation Makes a Real Impact

A monthly gift of $25 or even more turns you into a direct conservation partner—creating lasting change in one of the most threatened landscapes on Earth. Your support ensures:

Habitat Protection

Protection of nesting cliffs and habitats through direct collaboration with Quechua communities who steward the land and make key conservation decisions.

Forest Restoration

The establishment and maintenance of a 540,000 native tree nursery, restoring essential fruiting forests the macaws depend on.

Community Employment

Dignified local employment in ecotourism, honey production, and nursery work—offering sustainable alternatives to harmful land use and the illegal wildlife trade.

Your gift becomes tangible conservation action —protecting a species, empowering Indigenous communities, and reviving an ecosystem found nowhere else on the planet.
